Meet the team

Three engineers, one high bar.

Rob Froetscher

Founder

Rob loves working at the intersection of human and computer problems, striving for clear, concise solutions where humans let computers do what they do best. His projects add value immediately with minimal complexity and stay easy to change. He started in marketing, spent years as a growth engineer at Lumosity, then moved to data engineering — co-writing an open-source project to help employees access data and working on the company's big-data pipeline. He founded Brain Surprise in 2017 and has since served companies from healthcare to commodities to telecom.

Sam Marcellus

Engineer · ex-Uber

Sam cut his teeth writing C++ at financial-data company FactSet, building a solid foundation of technical and project-management skills. He then joined as an early engineer at Uber in 2013, and over five years worked on everything from driver payments to internal tooling, data infrastructure, privacy, and standing up Uber's first non-US data center in China. He's been both an individual contributor and an engineering manager, comfortable in technical leadership on small teams while staying close to engineering and product.

Andrew Xue

Engineer · ex-Goldman Sachs

Andy is a software engineer by way of the sciences — his first big project implemented a Monte Carlo model of DNA chains. He moved on to the demanding Program Trading Technology team at Goldman Sachs, where he led the Sales Trading platform and the sales-attribution system under real deadline pressure. He later joined Lumosity as one of its founding data engineers, scaling data infrastructure on Hadoop, Spark, and Kafka/Confluent. He has deep experience structuring systems to minimize complexity, equally at home with OOP, compositional, and functional styles.
